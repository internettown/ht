You receive experience for every released CPU, the higher rated the CPU, the more experience. You gain one level after getting 100 experience. Higher CPU levels decrease design time and are necessary to unlock new technologies.

Architectures are said to release with version 0.3 and any traces of them are simply placeholders - they have no effect.

You can learn more about this planned feature [here](https://itch.io/t/214142/planned-features).

## Clock Speed\[[](https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fhardware-tycoon.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FCPU%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D3&uselang=en&metadata=article-registration-edit-article-section "Sign in to edit")\]

Clock speed mainly affects a CPU's performance. Higher speeds usually mean more performance, lower stability[\[1\]](#cite_note-1), and higher build.

The lowest possible clock speed is 100 kHz, and 200 kHz is the default maximum after researching CPU Development.

See [R&D](/wiki/Research_%26_Development#CPU_Research "Research & Development") for all available max clock speed research.

Currently the maximum clock speed is 4.11 GHz, achievable with 100% build quality on a 1086 pin PGA (or better) in a 45 nm [technological process](/wiki/Technological_process "Technological process").

1.  [↑](#cite_ref-1) Stability is calculated relative to maximum and minimum clock speeds of the used tech process.

## Build Quality\[[](https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fhardware-tycoon.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FCPU%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D6&uselang=en&metadata=article-registration-edit-article-section "Sign in to edit")\]

The build quality is adjustable with a slider and can range from 50% to 100%. It affects the maximum clock speed (50% build quality is 66.66% clock frequency, 100% amounts to 103% max clock), the build stat of your CPU (50% build quality is 90%, 100% is 100% build), and the unit cost of your CPU.
